Reflections

Tyler Boyd - 16951977

I am currently in my third year of undergraduate study, working towards completing a Bachelor of Computer and Information Science majoring in Software Development. This paper has introduced me to a lot of new concepts that change the way I perceive my own interaction with computers. In this project, I learned how to properly conduct a usability test, and how to draw useful conclusions from it. I now feel like I have a solid understanding of why good usability is so important in technology. In a job such as Software development, having good typing speed and accuracy can increase the rate you work at drastically. As technology evolves, touch screen tablets such as the surface tablet used in our experiment are becoming increasingly common in both educational and workplace environments. Because of this, it is vital that touch screen tablets and other devices have good usability. In our experiment, a standard physical keyboard performed better on average than the touchscreen keyboard in both typing speed and accuracy. This evidently shows that a physical keyboard is easier to use than a touch screen keyboard. This confirmed my hypothesis from before the experiment was conducted.
Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed my experience working on this project and in this team.

Tarran O'Shaughnessy - 16951982

During my time with this project, I’ve managed to expand my knowledge on Human-Computer Interaction. Learning about how individual devices have an effect on basic haptic perception. In our case, the effect of using a standard keyboard versus a touch pad keyboard has given me an opportunity to learn that even though the layout is the same, the size and the ability to actually have a physical impact on a keyboard seems to have a direct effect on the speed of a user. Personally, I use a mechanical keyboard at home, and I feel more comfortable using that than a touch screen keyboard.

I feel like we could have improved our research a little bit. I think we didn’t really have a big enough test pool, meaning our results could be scrutinized a little bit. I also think that we should’ve chosen some people who aren’t studying BCIS to complete the test, it might have been a better way to diversify our pool a little bit.

My tasks during the project were to do most of the write up, meaning I wrote our methodology, variables, conclusion Etc. I also ensured that we had our documentation for getting participants to sign off on our ability to be able to use the data in whatever way we wanted. I also found the software that we would use in order to conduct our research.

Every member of the team was a pleasure to work with, everyone showed up to meetings and everyone had input on the direction of our research. Everyone has been more than willing to help each other out and complete their tasks on time without any issue.

Once I complete my degree as a software developer, learning about what keyboard is more suitable will help me understand what will allow me to be more productive when I go out to join the workforce. This project has given me some insight on how my knowledge fairs when it comes to statistical analysis, I struggled a little bit writing the conclusion more so in the way of writing a comprehensive analysis of what the results actually displayed. Due to this I’ve gained at least a little bit of knowledge on how to analyse and respond to hard data. Due to how good my team is and how well everyone worked together, I thoroughly enjoyed this project and its success.
